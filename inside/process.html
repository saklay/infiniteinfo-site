Infinite is seriously committed to delivering the world's best interactive media, on time, at affordable costs. To do this, we take a very disciplined approach to our work. We strongly believe that if we do our homework and get the planning and specifications done properly, we save a significant amount of time and effort and create a better product. We try to keep the process as simple as possible, getting clients involved at critical milestones. We use the following approach to managing projects:

Scope Phase: Project is scoped to become familiar with our clients' needs and the effort required implementing various aspects of the project. It includes:

  • Strategic planning and research
  • Needs assessment and goals
  • Project requirements
  • Competitive analysis

The usual output from this phase is the project outline or brief, a cost estimate, a rough schedule and a draft contract.

Specification Phase: In this phase Infinite produces the final specification and requirements document including:

  • Sample script or final specification
  • Technical requirements
  • Functionality
  • Material guidelines
  • Final production schedule
  • Three interface designs

Production Phase: This phase begins with Infinite's full-scale development of the project. It is followed by regular progress reviews (both internal and external client reviews at specific milestones) of work in progress.

Quality Assurance Phase: Although our applications are tested and stabilized at each milestone, there is a final testing to ensure quality from a technological and usability standpoint.

Rollout Phase: Release of finished application to client. Also includes web turnover document as needed.

Post Project Analysis: A summary of the project for internal use covering such areas as resource tracking and performance measurement.

Maintenance: This phase includes ongoing analysis, upgrades, updates, refreshes and future initiatives.
